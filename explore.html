FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Wildlife sightings are never guaranteed, as animals move freely in their natural habitat. However, visiting during the right season and time of day greatly increases your chances. Even if you don’t spot a particular species, the experience of being surrounded by nature’s beauty is always rewarding

Jordan is home to vast wildlife, the country is a very important migratory route for many bird species, the red sea is home to over 500 species of fish. Apart from the vast Jordanian wilderness, at a Seamonkey adventure, we try to maximize your chances of also spotting Arabian oryx, water buffalo, raptors, sea turtles, rock hyrax, desert foxes & Nubian ibex. There are animals that very few people have recorded in Jordan that are extremely difficult to find because of their very shy and skittish nature such as striped hyenas, sand cats, Eurasian badgers and whale sharks. With some great luck you could be some of the very few people that were able to spot them!

The best time to explore Jordan’s wildlife is spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November). During these months, the weather is mild, vegetation is greener, and animals are more active. In spring, you’ll also see migrating birds passing through the Jordan Valley and nature reserves. Summer can be very hot, especially in desert areas, and winter brings colder conditions that make spotting wildlife less comfortable.
